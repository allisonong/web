11/20/2020 #410 Wild Honey - The Beach Boys (1967)

“In 24 concise but utterly winning minutes, they romp through a set of low-fi sunbaked melodies and R&B and soul homages — all suffused with warmth, sly hooks, and a sense of band unity”.

Wild Honey is the shortest album on the list and that is exactly why I chose it for my first run. When I was a kid in Hong Kong my dad would give me his walkman and put in a Beach Boys CD (no idea which one) to occupy me. The Beach Boys I remember from childhood were a bunch of dudes crooning about California. When describing them to my husband at the dinner table, my impression consisted of a very high-pitched ‘oooooooh’ that didn’t help get my point across at all.

As I started my run I was not into it but over the course of the 24 minutes I went from unimpressed (top 500, really?), to confused (this isn’t how I thought the Beach Boys sound), to surprised (“I’d Love Just Once to See You”), to appreciative (okay this album is pretty fun). I’d never heard the Beach Boys sound like this before. At times they sound like The Rolling Stones and at times like the Beatles. But in the end (the last track ‘Mama Says’) they became The Beach Boys as I had known them and that ended up being the track I liked best. After 24 minutes of running I was wheezing. Running is feels hard and I feel slow.

About

When the Coronavirus blew up in March 2020, I had been training for a half marathon and looking forward to many outdoor adventures. Of course, my race, like everything else, was cancelled. After months of relative isolation and working from home, I was feeling depressed, out of breath walking up stairs, and getting squishy. Through it all, music and video games were there for me. (but unlike the video games, the music didn’t eventually make me more depressed) In October I came up with this project as a way to discover more music, force myself to get outside, exercise, and learn a new skill (I am writing this website from scratch).

Rules

  1. I will listen to one album on the list per run.
  2. The first time I listen to an album must be on a run. No pre-listening!
  3. After each run I'll document the run and my thoughts/experience.
